# BlinkCode Trademark Policy

## Purpose

BlinkCode is open-source software released under the Apache License 2.0. That
license gives broad permission to use, copy, modify, fork, and distribute the
source code.

The Apache License 2.0 does not automatically grant permission to use the
BlinkCode name, logo, icon, app branding, visual identity, official builds,
official release names, organization identifiers, or other project branding.
This policy explains how the BlinkCode brand may be used in ways that are clear,
truthful, and not confusing.

This document is a project policy, not legal advice. For formal trademark
protection, trademark registration, or legal interpretation, consult qualified
legal counsel.

## Protected Marks

The following are protected project identifiers for BlinkCode:

- The `BlinkCode` name.
- The `BlinkCodeOrg` GitHub organization name.
- The BlinkCode logo and logo variants.
- The BlinkCode app icon and icon variants.
- The BlinkCode visual identity used by the official app, website, screenshots,
  and release materials.
- Official app names, executable names, app identifiers, and package metadata,
  including `BlinkCode`, `blinkcode`, and `com.blinkcode.desktop`.
- Official release and installer names, including names such as
  `BlinkCode-Setup-<version>-x64.exe`, `BlinkCode-Portable-<version>-x64.exe`,
  `BlinkCode-<version>-<arch>.dmg`, `BlinkCode-<version>-<arch>.zip`,
  `BlinkCode-<version>-<arch>.AppImage`, and `BlinkCode-<version>-<arch>.deb`.
- Official project URLs and repositories, including
  `https://github.com/BlinkCodeOrg/BlinkCode`.
- Official extension and marketplace identifiers maintained by the project,
  including `blinkcode-extensions`, `blinkcode.markdown-preview`,
  `blinkcode.spell-checker`, and `blinkcode.theme-import`.

## Allowed Uses

You may use the BlinkCode name for truthful, non-confusing references to the
official project. Examples include:

- "Fork of BlinkCode"
- "Based on BlinkCode"
- "Compatible with BlinkCode"
- "Plugin for BlinkCode"
- "Integration for BlinkCode"
- Articles, tutorials, videos, reviews, documentation, and educational content
  that clearly refer to the official BlinkCode project.

You may use screenshots of the official BlinkCode app in GitHub projects, blog
posts, videos, tutorials, reviews, documentation, and educational content,
provided the use does not imply that your project, product, service, or content
is official, endorsed, sponsored, or approved by BlinkCode.

You may use the BlinkCode logo or app icon in community content such as videos,
articles, thumbnails, tutorials, and documentation when the use is clearly
referential and does not imply official status or endorsement.

Names such as "Awesome Theme for BlinkCode" or "Integration for BlinkCode" are
generally allowed when they describe compatibility and do not suggest that the
project is official.

## Uses That Require Permission

Permission is required for uses that may imply official status, endorsement,
sponsorship, approval, or affiliation with BlinkCode.

Examples of uses that require permission include:

- Using `BlinkCode` in the name of a fork, product, app, service, company,
  package, marketplace listing, or distribution in a way that may confuse users.
- Using the official BlinkCode logo, app icon, or visual identity as the primary
  branding for a separate product, service, fork, or modified build.
- Publishing builds that look like official BlinkCode builds.
- Using names such as `BlinkCode Pro`, `BlinkCode Enterprise`,
  `BlinkCode Cloud`, `BlinkCode Plus`, `BlinkCode Ultimate`,
  `BlinkCode Studio`, `BlinkCode AI`, `BlinkCode Official`,
  `Official BlinkCode`, or similar names that may suggest official status.
- Registering domains, social media accounts, package names, extension
  marketplace listings, app-store listings, or other public identifiers that
  include BlinkCode in a confusing way.
- Using the `BlinkCodeOrg` name outside a truthful reference to the official
  GitHub organization.

## Forks and Modified Versions

Forks and modified builds are allowed under the Apache License 2.0. However,
modified versions should avoid confusing users about what is official.

If you publish a fork or modified build, you should:

- Use a clearly different application name.
- Use different branding, logo, and app icon.
- Change the app identifier, executable name, installer names, and package
  metadata where needed to avoid confusion.
- Not present the build as an official BlinkCode release.
- Clearly state that the project is based on BlinkCode.
- Clearly state that the build is modified and unofficial.
- Remove or replace official BlinkCode branding where needed to avoid confusion.

You may describe your project as "based on BlinkCode" or "a fork of BlinkCode",
but the product name itself should not use `BlinkCode` unless permission is
granted. Names such as `BlinkCode Community Fork` may be acceptable only if the
context is clearly non-official and non-confusing, but a distinct project name is
strongly preferred.

## Plugins, Extensions, and Integrations

Third-party plugins, extensions, integrations, themes, and tools may describe
compatibility with BlinkCode. Allowed descriptive names include:

- "Plugin for BlinkCode"
- "Compatible with BlinkCode"
- "Integration for BlinkCode"
- "Theme for BlinkCode"

Names that imply official status require permission. Examples include:

- `BlinkCode Official Plugin`
- `BlinkCode Pro Extension`
- `BlinkCode Enterprise Integration`
- `Official BlinkCode Theme`

The `blinkcode.*` extension identifier namespace is reserved for extensions that
are part of the BlinkCode project, accepted into a BlinkCode-maintained
community marketplace, or otherwise approved by BlinkCode maintainers. Other
extension authors should use their own publisher namespace, such as
`publisher.extension-name`.

The `bcode` command name may be used for BlinkCode extension tooling and
community extension workflows when the use is not confusing and does not imply
that a third-party tool is official.

## Community Content

Community content is welcome. You may create screenshots, tutorials, videos,
blog posts, reviews, documentation, guides, and educational material about
BlinkCode.

Community content should not imply that it is official, sponsored, endorsed, or
approved by BlinkCode unless you have permission. If there is any chance of
confusion, include a short clarification such as "Unofficial guide" or "Not
affiliated with BlinkCode."

## No Endorsement

Using the BlinkCode name for truthful reference does not imply endorsement,
sponsorship, approval, partnership, or affiliation with BlinkCode, BlinkCodeOrg,
or the project maintainers.
